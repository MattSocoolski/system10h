# European PBN distribution: market map and target list for ArtNapi

**ArtNapi has a viable path to pan-European distribution through approximately 40 identified B2B distributors across 14 markets, with 10 high-priority targets ready for immediate outreach.** The European paint-by-numbers market is structurally split: Ravensburger dominates the children's segment everywhere, Schipper leads premium adult PBN in German-speaking markets, and a growing wave of Chinese-sourced D2C brands fills the mid-range online. ArtNapi's competitive positioning — **470+ designs, direct Chinese factory sourcing, competitive pricing, and private-label capability** — fits a clear gap between expensive German incumbents and cheap unbranded imports. The priority entry points are Germany (largest market), Benelux (strongest hobby wholesale infrastructure), Scandinavia (high purchasing power, no local PBN brand), and Czech Republic (geographic proximity, active market).

## Margin structure for PBN distribution in Europe

For a PBN kit with an RRP of approximately **€29.95**, the economics break down as follows:

| Stage | Typical price | Margin on purchase | Share of RRP |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Manufacturing cost (ex-factory China) | **€5–7** | — | 17–23% |
| Manufacturer → Distributor | **€10–12** | 40–70% markup on COGS | 33–40% |
| Distributor → Retailer | **€13–16** | **25–35% markup** (20–35% gross margin) | 43–53% |
| Retailer → Consumer (RRP) | **€29.95** | **87–130% markup** (45–55% gross margin) | 100% |

For a **direct-to-retailer model** (bypassing the distributor), ArtNapi could sell at €12–15 per kit to retailers, improving its own margin while still offering retailers their expected 50% gross margin on a €29.95 RRP.
